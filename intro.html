Computer programming language, any of the various languages for a digital computer to communicate a series of comprehensive instructions. These instructions may be executed directly when they are in the computer-specific numerical form known as the machine language, after a simple process of substitution when represented in a corresponding assembly language, or after translation from any "higher level" language. While computer languages are numerous, very few are commonly used.Machine and assembly languages are "low-level" which requires a programmer to specifically handle all the idiosyncratic data storage and operation features of a device. On the other hand, high-level languages protect a programmer from thinking about these issues, and have a syntax that is easier for programmers to write and read.
